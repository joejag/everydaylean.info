]]> By: Sheldon Hearn http://everydaylean.info/2013/04/contractors-consultants-coaches/#comment-166 Wed, 25 Sep 2013 07:11:56 +0000 http://everydaylean.info/?p=2826#comment-166 I like substituting the word assist for help.

It is very easy to associate “help” with “problem”.

You need help because something is wrong (with you?). If you are incapable of solving the problem, you admit it by asking for help, and the solution comes from the helper.

However, it’s unnatural to talk about assisting someone with a problem.

When I think of assistance, I think of someone contributing to *my* effort to solve the problem. If I’m capable of solving the problem, I ask for assistance, and support comes from the assistant.

When the managing director of a company takes on an assistant, the share price does not plummet. Nor does having an assistant undermine her status.

Maybe it’s just a word game, but I find that avoiding the word “help” in a supporting relationship, creates a better context for empowerment.
